Problem

Video content is global by default. A single season or campaign now requires many trailers, cutdowns, shorts, social promos and hundreds of image variants across platforms, languages and territories. Each asset needs localized text, ratings, logos and legal elements and must follow regional and platform rules.

Today, this work is stitched together with a patchwork of tools, agencies and spreadsheets. New video generators and design tools increase the volume of assets but add even more links, files and risk around compliance, rights and consistency. There is no single place that understands the full title graph, what exists, what is missing, what passed which checks and what is truly ready for release.

Teams spend too much time on manual ops and rework and not enough time on creative ideas, experimentation and new formats.

Product

Vidcomply is a one-stop release platform that combines title-aware compliance, asset generation and localization, and release planning into a single layer sitting on top of the tools you already use.

You can upload existing assets or generate new ones from a brief. Vidcomply handles full episodes and movies, trailers, CTV spots, shorts and social edits, and all the supporting art like posters and key visuals.

1. Compliance engine

  • Checks full episodes, promos, shorts and key art against regional and platform rules.
  • Flags risky moments and missing elements (ratings bugs, logos, disclaimers, sensitive scenes).
  • Outputs editor timelines and legal-ready summaries that plug into existing review workflows.
  • Outcome: reduces rejections by 40% and delays by 20%.

2. Marketing asset studio

  • Starts from a title or campaign brief plus reference assets.
  • Repurposes existing content or creates new trailers, shorts and posters via connected tools and generators.
  • Localizes and reformats variants per platform and territory, always tied back to compliance and lineage.

3. Release planner

  • Derives the required asset list for each title by platform, market and window.
  • Breaks work into concrete operations: reuse, edit or generate from scratch.
  • Tracks readiness, approvals and delivery status like a release CRM — but for actual files, rules and tools.

The goal is not to replace your stack but to connect it, enforce governance and free teams from operational drag so they can spend more time on creativity and experimentation.

How it fits into existing workflows

Vidcomply sits above storage, MAM/DAM (Media/Digital Asset Management), NLE/VFX (Non-Linear Editing/Visual Effects) and AI services as the release and governance layer. It pulls in what already exists, plans what is missing, and routes work to the right tools and teams.

  • Pulls metadata and assets from existing libraries and project systems.
  • Calls out to connected generators and creative tools when new assets are needed.
  • Pushes approved assets and timelines back to delivery, ad ops and partners.

For marketing, legal and ops teams, Vidcomply behaves like a title- and campaign-centric “release OS”: a single view of assets, checks and readiness across regions and platforms, rather than yet another siloed point tool.

Data flywheel

Every title and campaign makes the system smarter. Vidcomply continuously learns which formats, clearances and fixes are needed for different platforms, genres and locales.

  • Patterns in rejections and late fixes feed back into earlier checks and templates.
  • Successful campaigns inform default asset plans and localization strategies.
  • Compliance results, lineage and prompts become structured data that improves future planning and automation.

Over time this creates a proprietary data asset: a living map of what is required to safely release different kinds of content around the world, and how to get there with less friction.

Why now

Generative tools make content creation cheaper and faster and increase the number of assets per title and campaign. At the same time, rules around content safety, disclosures and platform policies are tightening in every major market.

Creative tools and models are not the bottleneck. The bottleneck is orchestration, governance and packaging for global release. Vidcomply focuses on that layer rather than competing with the tools. Unlike point tools (e.g., Adobe Sensei for edits, or standalone compliance like Frame.io), Vidcomply orchestrates the full stack—creating a proprietary 'release map' dataset as our moat.
