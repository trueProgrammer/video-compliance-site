Avid native integration

VidComply writes directly into your Pro Tools session as clip gain automation — the same way a senior mixer would. Sessions arrive in any ingest format and leave as a structured AAF, timecode-locked, stems routed, ready for your console.

Crosstalk rejection

Thirty open lavs. One active speaker. The other twenty-nine channels are feeding your mix with bleed, phase cancellation, and comb filtering. VidComply runs comparative waveform analysis across every ISO simultaneously — attenuating inactive sources with sub-millisecond precision, leaving only the dominant signal untouched.

ISO track organisation

Mic assignments are inconsistent across every episode — there is no template that survives production. VidComply fingerprints each source, resolves channel identity dynamically, and routes every ISO into its correct stem: Dialogue, M&E, SFX, or Ambience. The session arrives at the console pre-routed.

Clip gain staging

Pre-fader normalization applied at the clip level — not on the fader, not destructively. Every whisper, every shout, every inconsistent recording level lands at a calibrated starting point before it reaches the console. The mixer makes creative decisions, not corrective ones.

Broadcast normalisation

Eliminate hours of manual Pro Tools labor per episode. VidComply scans the completed master using ITU-R BS.1770, applies the required level change, and exports a compliant broadcast or streaming deliverable without reopening the mix session. True Peak limits stay controlled without destructive pumping.
